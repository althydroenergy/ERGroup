Innovative Small Hydropower Project

Decentralized Clean Energy from Irrigation Canals

Project Overview

The ERGroup Small Hydropower Project is a pilot micro-hydropower installation located in the Kibray district of Tashkent region, Uzbekistan. The system is installed directly on an existing irrigation canal without the construction of dams or alteration of water flow regimes.

The pilot unit has already been installed and tested using the team's own resources. Current output reaches up to 5 kW, with planned expansion to 8–10 kW.

Technology

The system uses a free-flow hydro turbine optimized for low-head and moderate-flow irrigation channels. The design focuses on:

  • High energy extraction efficiency
  • Minimal environmental impact
  • No need for dam construction
  • Low operational and maintenance costs

The turbine geometry is based on proprietary research aimed at improving power output by 20–30% compared to conventional free-flow systems.

Why It Matters

Uzbekistan has thousands of kilometers of irrigation canals with stable water flow. Installing micro-hydropower units on suitable sections can provide:

  • Distributed renewable electricity
  • Reduced CO₂ emissions
  • Energy support for rural communities
  • Scalable decentralized infrastructure

Environmental Impact

The project operates without blocking fish migration, without modifying hydrological regimes, and without flooding land areas. It represents a low-carbon and environmentally safe energy solution.

Scalability

The pilot installation serves as a demonstration model. After optimization, the design can be replicated on similar irrigation canals across Uzbekistan and other countries with comparable hydraulic infrastructure.

Current Status

  • Pilot turbine installed
  • Generator and inverter connected
  • Operational testing completed
  • Optimization phase ongoing
