Frequently Asked Questions

Why is your unit more favorable than solar panels?
Our water turbine is even cleaner and cheaper than solar energy as it does not depend on weather conditions, the time of the day or season and can operate 24/7 at full capacity.
Is it environmentally friendlier than solar panels?
Compared to solar power stations (SPS), hydraulic turbines also have additional environmental advantages, as they do not have "consumable" components, such as batteries and solar panels, which are the main equipment in SPSs. They have a limited service life due to degradation of these components over time. Their production and disposal is also less environmentally friendly.
What is the power capacity of your turbine?
The approximate power capacity of the smallest unit is 3 kW. This allows the generation of more than 70 kWh of electricity per day, which is enough to power two to three large houses. In this case only additional technical means are needed to compensate for peak loads.
Can one such unit supply electricity to a farm or a whole village?
Larger hydropower units can have a capacity of up to 200–300 kW, which is enough to supply a farm, village or settlement. This is possible on channels with a depth of at least 2 meters and a width of 10 meters or more. Annual power generation can reach 1.5–2 GWh. It is also possible to install a cascade of several small modules connected to one power plant. Such units can be placed at a distance of at least 100 m from each other.
What makes your turbine different from existing similar ones?
Our turbine’s diagram may resemble a well-known waterwheel, which is inefficient at low flow velocities. Our turbine consists of two such wheels connected in a special design that allows them to be used much more efficiently in slow water flow. For more details see the scientific publication:

https://erg.ucoz.org/ISJAEE-12-2010-eng.pdf
Will floating debris hamper the operation of the turbine?
A protective grid installed in front of the turbine will drive off branches, chunks, animal corpses and other major debris. Modern hydropower technology already offers well-developed protection methods. Fine debris will pass through the turbine without affecting operation.
Will the turbine harm fish?
Our turbine does not harm river fauna because it does not block the entire channel and rotates only about 20–30 times per minute, which is dozens of times slower than boat propellers or conventional hydro turbines. For fish it is similar to a natural obstacle such as a small island or waterfall. The turbine also aerates the water, which benefits aquatic ecosystems.
Would several turbines hinder each other if installed in one stream section?
It is possible to install a cascade of several modules connected to one power plant. They can be placed at distances of at least 100 meters and do not significantly influence each other.
How much electricity does it produce at a flow of 1 m/s, 2 m/s and higher?
Standard free-flow turbines are efficient only at water velocities above about 2 m/s. At a speed of 1 m/s their efficiency drops dramatically. Our turbine is designed to operate efficiently in slower water flows below 2 m/s.
Does it work in all weather conditions? What happens during floods?
The turbine floats in the water and its key components are waterproof. Rain does not affect operation. When the river level rises during floods, the turbine remains floating on the surface and continues to operate.
What is the possible market size of your machine?
Potential clients include farmers, businesses, private households and governments interested in developing renewable energy and improving electricity supply in remote settlements.
What is the cost of the turbine?
The estimated cost is about $2000–3000 per kW depending on configuration. Final cost will be determined after the pilot project and scaling of production. The expected payback period is about 2–3 years.
What are the turbine's disadvantages?
River or canal depth is critical for efficiency. Calculations show that turbines are less efficient in streams with depths below about 1 meter and flow velocities below 1.5 m/s. The optimal turbine height is around 2 meters to achieve the design capacity.
Where will the pilot project be implemented?
We are open to proposals from any country for implementing the pilot project. The site must satisfy the technical requirements described in the section “Other Ways You Can Help”. Visa support, accommodation and working conditions for the project team may also be required.
How will the funds raised during the campaign be used?
Funds will be used to implement a pilot project and to scale the production of energy modules. More details are provided in the section “How We'll Use the Money”.
