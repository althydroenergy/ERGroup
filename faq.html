Frequently Asked Questions

Why is your unit more favorable than solar panels?
Our water turbine is even cleaner and cheaper than solar energy as it does not depend on weather conditions, time of day, or season and can operate 24/7 at full capacity.
Last update: 09.03.2024
Is it environmentally friendlier than solar panels?
Compared to solar power stations, hydraulic turbines have additional environmental advantages because they do not require consumable components such as batteries and solar panels which degrade over time and must be replaced.
Last update: 09.03.2024
What is the power capacity of your turbine?
The approximate power capacity of the smallest unit is about 3 kW. This allows generation of more than 70 kWh of electricity per day, which is enough to power two to three large houses.
Last update: 12.03.2024
Can one such unit supply electricity to a farm or a village?
Larger hydropower units can reach capacities of 200–300 kW which is enough to supply a farm, village or settlement. A cascade of several modules can also be installed along a channel at distances of about 100 meters.
Last update: 09.03.2024
What makes your turbine different from existing technologies?
Our turbine uses a tandem wheel configuration that allows efficient energy extraction even at low water velocities. Scientific background is described here:

https://erg.ucoz.org/ISJAEE-12-2010-eng.pdf
Last update: 14.03.2024
Will floating debris affect turbine operation?
A protective grid installed upstream prevents branches and large debris from entering the turbine. Fine debris can pass through without affecting operation.
Last update: 14.03.2024
Will the turbine harm fish?
The turbine rotates slowly (20–30 rpm) and does not block the entire channel. For fish it behaves similarly to a natural obstacle such as a small island or waterfall. Additionally it aerates the water which benefits aquatic ecosystems.
Last update: 13.03.2024
Can several turbines operate in the same river section?
Yes. Several modules can be installed in a cascade with spacing of around 100 meters without influencing each other.
Last update: 11.03.2024
Does it work in all weather conditions?
The turbine is mounted on a floating pontoon and follows water level changes. Rain or floods do not stop operation because the turbine floats with the river surface.
Last update: 14.03.2024
What is the cost of the turbine?
The expected cost is about $2000–3000 per kW depending on configuration. Payback time is estimated at approximately 2–3 years.
