Frequently Asked Questions

▶ 1. Why is your unit more favorable than solar panels?
Our water turbine does not depend on weather conditions, the time of day, or season and can operate 24/7 at full capacity.
▶ 2. Is it environmentally friendlier than solar panels?
Hydraulic turbines do not require consumable components such as batteries and solar panels which degrade over time and must be replaced.
▶ 3. What is the power capacity of your turbine?
The smallest unit produces about 3 kW and can generate more than 70 kWh per day which is enough to power two to three houses.
▶ 4. Can one unit supply electricity to a farm or village?
Large units can reach capacities of 200–300 kW which is sufficient for farms, villages, or settlements.
▶ 5. What makes your turbine different from existing technologies?
Our turbine uses a tandem wheel design allowing efficient energy extraction in slow water flow.

▶ 6. Will floating debris affect turbine operation?
A protective grid prevents large debris from entering the turbine while small particles pass through safely.
▶ 7. Will the turbine harm fish?
The turbine rotates slowly (20–30 rpm) and does not block the channel, making it safe for fish.
▶ 8. Can several turbines operate in one river section?
Several modules can be installed in a cascade at distances of about 100 meters without affecting each other.
▶ 9. How much electricity is produced at different flow speeds?
Conventional turbines require flows above 2 m/s, while our design maintains efficiency even below this velocity.
▶ 10. Does it work in all weather conditions?
The turbine floats on a pontoon and automatically follows river level changes.
▶ 11. What is the possible market size?
Potential clients include farmers, businesses, rural households and governments developing renewable energy.
▶ 12. What is the cost of the turbine?
Estimated cost is about $2000–3000 per kW with a payback time of about 2–3 years.
▶ 13. What are the turbine disadvantages?
A river or canal depth of about 2 meters is required to reach optimal performance.
▶ 14. Where will the pilot project be implemented?
We are open to proposals from any country if the site meets technical requirements.
▶ 15. How will the raised funds be used?
Funds will be used to implement a pilot project and launch production of energy modules.
