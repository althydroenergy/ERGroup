Frequently Asked Questions

1 Why is your unit more favorable than solar panels? ▶
Our water turbine is even cleaner and cheaper than solar energy as it does not depend on weather conditions, the time of the day or season and can operate 24/7 at full capacity.
2 Is it environmentally friendlier than solar panels? ▶
Compared to solar power stations (SPS), hydraulic turbines also have additional environmental advantages, as they do not have "consumable" components, such as batteries and solar panels, which are the main equipment in SPSs. They have a limited service life due to degradation of these components over time. And their production and utilization is not environmentally-friendly.
3 What is the power capacity of your turbine? ▶
The approximate power capacity of the smallest unit is 3 kW. This allows the generation of more than 70 kWh of electricity per day, which is enough to power two to three large houses.In this case, only additional technical means are needed to compensate for peak loads.
4 Can one such unit supply electricity to a farm or a whole village? ▶
Larger hydropower units can have a capacity of up to 200-300 kW, which is enough to supply a farm, village, or a settlement. This is possible on channels with a depth of at least 2 meters and a width of 10 meters or more. In this case, annual power generation can reach 1.5-2 GWh. It is also possible to install a cascade of several small modules connected to one power plant. Such units can be placed at a distance of at least 100 m from each other.
5 What makes your turbine different from existing similar ones? What are the advantages of your turbine over existing similar technologies? ▶
Our turbine's diagram may be reminiscent of a well-known waterwheel, which is inefficient at low flow velocities. Our turbine consists of two such wheels connected in a special design that allows them to be used much more efficiently in slow water flow. For more details, please see our scientific work that proves high efficiency of such tandem design:

6 Will floating debris hamper the operation of the turbine? ▶
A protective web installed in front of the turbine will drive off branches, chunks, animal corpse, and other major debries from the turbine. The modern hydropower industry offers various well-developed protection techniques and it will not be a big challenge. Fine debris will flow through the turbine without impeding its work.
7 Will the turbine harm fish? ▶
Our turbine does not harm the fauna of rivers as it does not completely block the channel and makes only about 20-30 rotations per minute, which is dozens of times slower than the propellers of boats, ships, or traditional hydro turbines. For fish, this turbine will be similar to a natural obstacle, such as a small island or a waterfall. On the contrary, our turbine will additionally aerate the water which is beneficial for river fauna and flora.
8 Would they slow the flow/hinder one another if several pieces operate in one section of stream? ▶
It is possible to install a cascade of several small modules connected to one power plant. Such units can be placed at a distance of at least 100 m from each other and do not influence each other in any way.
9 How much electricity does it produceat a current flow of 1 m/s, 2m/s and so on? ▶
Standard free-flow turbines are efficient only with water flow speeds of 2 m/s and higher. At a speed of 1 m/s, their efficiency decreases eightfold! Our turbine has high efficiency in watercourses slower than 2 m/s. More detailed information is available in the text.
10 Does it work in all weather conditions? How can floods affect the turbine? ▶
The turbine floats in water and its key components are waterproof and rain cannot is not an obstacle to its work. The turbine is fixed to pontoon and when the river's water level raises, the turbine remains floating on the water surface and continue to work.
11 What's the possible market size of your machine? ▶
Our clients will include farmers, businesses, and private households across the world and governments interested in improving remote settlements and develop renewables.
12 What is the cost of the turbine? ▶
Our hydropower turbine's estimated cost will be calculated as $2,000-3,000 per 1 kW depending on configuration. The cost will be determined after the completion of a pilot project and during the scale-up. The turbine's cost is expected to be paid off within 2-3 years of operation. More economic estimates are given in the main text.
13 What are the turbine's disadvantages? ▶
The depth of the river or canal is critical for the turbine's efficiency. The turbine's minimal height should be 2 meters as calculations show that such turbines' efficiency is low in streams with a depth of less than 1 meter and water flow velocity less than 1.5 m/sec. The turbine's minimal height should be 2 meters to achieve its design capacity.
