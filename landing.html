Is There a Betz Limit for Open-Channel Flow?

Short answer: Yes.

An ideal free-surface (open-channel) flow possesses a universal energy extraction limit, analogous in role to the Betz limit for wind turbines.

The Key Result

  • Extremal Froude number: Fr ≈ 0.3094
  • Maximum extractable energy: η ≈ 34.52%
  • Regime: strictly subcritical (Fr < 1)

The limit arises from analytical energy optimization and does not depend on friction laws or turbulence models.

Main result:
The extractable energy Φ(Fr) exhibits a universal subcritical maximum, defining a fundamental energetic bound for open-channel flow.

Why This Matters

  • Clarifies the physical role of the Froude number
  • Separates geometric limits from technological efficiency
  • Provides a benchmark for hydrokinetic device design
