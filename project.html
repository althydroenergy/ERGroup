Short Summary

In an era marked by political instability, risks of a global energy crisis, soaring fuel prices and an urgent worldwide push to reduce CO₂ emissions, the demand for renewable energy solutions has never been more pronounced.

Our project proposes the construction and mass production of free-flow micro-hydroelectric power plants that do not require dams or blocking rivers. These turbines can operate efficiently in slow water currents, opening a huge new market for renewable energy generation.

Project Story

The world is rapidly transitioning away from fossil fuels. By 2050, renewable energy sources such as solar, wind and hydropower are expected to provide more than half of global electricity generation.

Traditional hydropower stations require dams and significant infrastructure. However, millions of rivers and irrigation canals around the world do not have sufficient pressure head for conventional hydropower.

Our innovative turbine is designed specifically for such locations.

Unlike many existing systems that require fast water flows greater than 2 m/s, our turbine works efficiently even in slower currents due to a specific hydrodynamic effect developed during previous research and development.

This effect allows the turbine to convert sub-critical flow into critical flow conditions, significantly increasing energy extraction efficiency.

According to research results, the efficiency increase compared with similar devices may reach **60–80%**. 0

What We Need & What You Get

To build the pilot project and prepare for serial production, we require approximately $150,000 for a one-year development program.

The first stage will include engineering design, prototype development and testing.

The smallest turbine unit will have a capacity of about 3 kW, capable of generating more than 70 kWh of electricity per day, enough to power two or three houses.

Larger units could reach capacities of 200–300 kW, sufficient to power farms, villages or small settlements.

Multiple turbines can also be installed in cascades along canals or rivers.

The Impact

Farmers and rural communities often face unstable electricity supply for irrigation and domestic needs.

Free-flow micro hydropower can provide reliable electricity directly at the point of use.

Unlike solar or wind power, hydropower systems can operate continuously 24 hours per day without dependence on weather conditions.

Our turbine rotates slowly (20–30 RPM), making it safe for fish and aquatic ecosystems. It also aerates the water, improving environmental conditions.

Risks & Challenges

Possible risks include engineering challenges during prototype development and fluctuations in material costs.

These risks are mitigated by involving experienced engineers and by using widely available materials and manufacturing techniques.

Demand for decentralized renewable energy technologies continues to grow worldwide, which significantly reduces market risks.

FAQ

Does the turbine require a dam?

No. The turbine works in natural river or canal flow.

Is the turbine environmentally safe?

Yes. Slow rotation and open flow design minimize impact on aquatic life.

Where can it be installed?

On rivers, irrigation canals and water channels with moderate flow.
