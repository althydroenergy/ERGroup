Scientific Basis

The operation principle of the turbine is based on hydrodynamic transformation of open-channel flow regimes. In particular, the turbine induces a transition of the flow toward the critical regime where the total specific energy of the stream is minimal.

Theoretical analysis shows that the optimal condition occurs near the dimensionless parameter known as the Froude number:

Fr ≈ 0.3094

Under these conditions the flow reaches a configuration where the extractable portion of mechanical energy from the stream approaches a universal limit analogous to the Betz limit known in wind energy.

For open-channel flow this theoretical limit corresponds to approximately:

34.52 % of the total flow energy

Short Summary

Our project proposes the construction and mass‑production of free‑flow micro‑hydroelectric power plants (micro HPPs) which do not require dams.

The turbine is optimized for slow rivers and irrigation canals where conventional turbines are inefficient.
