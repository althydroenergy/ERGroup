Universal Energy Extraction Limit in Open-Channel Flow

This page presents a universal energetic limit for ideal free-surface flows, derived analytically from first principles.

Core Result

The extractable energy of an ideal incompressible open-channel flow exhibits a subcritical extremum at the universal Froude number:

Frext ≈ 0.3094

This extremum represents a fundamental energy limit that does not depend on friction, channel roughness, or empirical resistance laws (Manning, Chezy, etc.).

Energy Limit (Betz-Type Analogy)

In wind energy theory, the Betz limit establishes a universal upper bound on extractable kinetic energy:

ηBetz = 16/27 ≈ 59.3%

For open-channel free-surface flow, an analogous universal constraint arises from energy balance and flow continuity.

Maximum extractable energy fraction:

ηOCF ≈ 34.52%

This value emerges naturally from the dimensionless energy function Φ(Fr) and is attained precisely at:

Fr ≈ 0.3094

Thus, Fr ≈ 0.3094 plays the same theoretical role for open-channel flow as the Betz limit does for wind turbines.

Physical Interpretation

  • The extremum occurs strictly in the subcritical regime (Fr < 1).
  • At Fr → 1, hydrostatic pressure support degenerates and extractable energy vanishes.
  • The limit is geometric and energetic in nature, not technological.
  • No turbulence modeling or dissipation assumptions are involved.
